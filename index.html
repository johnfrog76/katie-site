Bio

Katie Webster is an alto saxophonist, improviser, composer, and educator from Seattle. A 2022 graduate of the Berklee College of Music, she is now pursuing her master’s degree at the Berklee Global Jazz Institute. Some of Katie’s performance highlights include the Bern International Jazz Festival, Vermont Jazz Festival, Monterey Jazz Festival, Lincoln Center, Songbyrd Music House, and numerous venues around the Seattle and Boston area as a bandleader and side person.

In October 2022, Webster was commissioned by the Mass Art Art Museum to write and perform a composition inspired by the life and contributions of Elizabeth Mumbet Freeman in collaboration with Joana Vasconcelos’ ‘The Valkyrie,’ a large-scale installation premiered at the museum. Webster has performed as a featured soloist with the Seattle Repertory Jazz Orchestra. In June 2022, Katie received a full scholarship to attend the Keep an Eye Summer Jazz Workshop at the Conservatorium van Amsterdam. At the CvA she was selected to perform in the CvA Large Ensemble conducted by Nils van Haften with featured artist Dick Oatts. She is the 22nd Jimmy Lyons Full Tuition Scholarship recipient presented by Berklee in conjunction with Monterey Jazz Festival — and has made history as the first woman to win the award. Katie was accepted for several collaborative musical programs including Terri Lyne Carrington’s Institute of Jazz and Gender Justice, and the Global Jazz Institute, where she had the opportunity to learn from celebrated musicians Kris Davis, Terri Lyne Carrington, Linda May Han Oh, Danilo Perez, Joe Lovano, and Val Jeanty. During her time at Berklee, Katie was fortunate to have studied with Tony Malaby, George Garzone, Godwin Louis and Francisco Mela.

As an educator, Katie taught as a clinician at the Vermont Jazz Festival, Auburn High School Jazz Festival in Auburn, WA., and alongside Gary Hammon at The Annual Ballard High School Jazz Workshop in Seattle. In 2021, she was a part of Kris Davis’ education outreach program through the Institute of Jazz and Gender Justice. As a representative of the program, she frequently traveled to the Boston Arts Academy to work with high school students. Webster currently resides in Boston, where she studies and performs around the east coast.
